What is Type Hierarchy?

Type hierarchy is how we organize our content through the structure and design of our type. Strong type hierarchy makes it easy to read, understand, and navigate through our page content.

At its simplest, good type hierarchy has strong design for the different heading levels, making them easy to distinguish between. With good headings, you can quickly scan through the page and see what content is grouped and how it relates to each other.

On the web, good type hierarchy has two basic steps to be successful. The first is to implement proper html heading structure (h1, h2, h3, etc.). This provides the foundation and has positive benefits for SEO and accessibility. It also lays the ground work for easily implementing heading design styles. Once your html heading structure is correct, the next step is to map your heading styles to the appropriate tags. If your html structure is strong, you should be good to go!
